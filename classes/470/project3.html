project 3 blog post

Background

The goal of project 3 was to create some sort of an artistic experience.

Building custom lighting fixtures has always been a dream of mine- and with the doors that have been opened in my head with arduinos and microcontrollers as a whole. The amount of libraries and dependencies that have been created for IOT devices and small microcontrollers is incredible!

What to Make?

One of my favorite lighting fixtures is an LED tube light. I had always wanted one so badly when I started to get more into lighting. Well, to backtrack I was really into photography and videography for a while- and tube lights were almost a standard for wash lighting and as I started to learn more about stage lighting and programmed a few of my own shows, tube lights and pixel fixtures are incredibly neat! (and being used much more often in shows nowadays)

The Big Issue: Cost

Most tubes are insanely expensive, upwards of hundreds of dollars. While the lumen output and color reproduction is likely incredible, I wanted to try and make one for significantly less. You can find tube light covers designed for protecting larger industrial tube lights, and theyre $3 and fairly well built! For diffusion, parchment paper works pretty well with LED strips and smaller led pixels. I folded the paper and fit in in the tube, and then added an argb led strip. I had tried this with a 12v rgb led strip and transistors, but found that it was much easier to use a 5v argb led stip and directly control the pixels with wled, an incredibly feature rich esp32 library for controlling LEDs!

Changing Direction

The primary decision for using a 5v argb strip was so that I could natively use the WLED library for driving pixels rather than have to use a dedicated artnet decoder. This would also allow me to use SPI rather than just RGB control for the whole strip! Meaning I can have control over each pixel on the strip rather than just one fixed color for every pixel on the strip. Perhaps the most important library I used was the WLED Library . This allowed me to have control over various settings over a web server and also allowed me to wirelessly transmit artnet packets over wifi from resolume arena, or any other lighting software of my choice.

Resolume Controlling Wirelessly!

What's Next

I would like to build more of these, and I would also like to build a wired ethernet version that would be a lot more reliable in show conditions. Wifi is too unstable and packet loss is inevitable. Having a wired version would make operation much more efficient and simpler. I have a WT32-eth01 sitting around that would be perfect for this role. Or even with a different route, I could have a dmx decoder on each fixture that would convert dmx to spi, and could be more of a modular setup. I feel like there are many more directions that I could take this project to make it more of a standalone product that can be integrated into real-world show environments.
